The analysis of a curious metal ornament found in the tomb of Chou-Chu, a military leader in 3rd century China, turned out to be 85% aluminium. How it was produced remains a mystery. By the end of the 1700s, aluminium oxide was known to contain a metal, but it defeated all attempts to extract it.Aluminium is a silvery-white, lightweight metal. It is soft and malleable.Aluminium is the most abundant metal in the Earth’s crust (8.1%) but is rarely found uncombined in nature.It is usually found in minerals such as bauxite and cryolite.
