Indium was discovered in 1863 by Ferdinand Reich at the Freiberg School of Mines in Germany. Reich was investigating a sample of the mineral zinc blende (now known as sphalerite, ZnS) which he believed might contain the recently discovered element thallium. It is a soft, silvery metal that is stable in air and water. Indium is one of the least abundant minerals on Earth. It has been found uncombined in nature, but typically it is found associated with zinc minerals and iron, lead and copper ores. It is commercially produced as a by-product of zinc refining.
