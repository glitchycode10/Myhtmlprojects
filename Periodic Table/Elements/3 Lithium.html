Lithium

Standard State Solid
Atomic Mass 7.0 u
Electron Configuration [He]2s1
Oxidation States +1
Electronegativity (Pauling Scale) 0.98
Atomic Radius (van der Waals) 182 pm
Ionization Energy 5.392 eV
Electron Affinity 0.618 eV
Melting Point 453.65 K
Boiling Point 1615 K
Density 0.534 g/cm³
Year discovered 1817


The first lithium mineral petalite, LiAlSi4O10, was discovered on the Swedish island of Utö by the Brazilian, Jozé Bonifácio de Andralda e Silva in the 1790s. It was observed to give an intense crimson flame when thrown onto a fire.It is a soft, silvery metal. It has the lowest density of all metals. It reacts vigorously with water.Lithium does not occur as the metal in nature, but is found combined in small amounts in nearly all igneous rocks and in the waters of many mineral springs.Most lithium is currently produced in Chile.
