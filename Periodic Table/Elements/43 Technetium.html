Technetium

Standard State Solid
Atomic Mass 96.90636 u
Electron Configuration [Kr]5s24d5
Oxidation States +7,+6,+4
Electronegativity (Pauling Scale) 1.9
Atomic Radius (van der Waals) 209 pm
Ionization Energy 7.28 eV
Electron Affinity 0.55 eV
Melting Point 2430 K
Boiling Point 4538 K
Density 11 g/cm³
Year discovered 1937


Technetium was discovered by Emilio Segrè in 1937 in Italy. He investigated molybdenum from California which had been exposed to high energy radiation and he found technetium to be present and separated it. Today, this element is extracted from spent nuclear fuel rods in tonne quantities. It is a radioactive, silvery metal that does not occur naturally.The metal is produced in tonne quantities from the fission products of uranium nuclear fuel. It is obtained as a grey powder.
