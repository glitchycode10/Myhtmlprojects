Dysprosium

Standard State Solid
Atomic Mass 1.0080 u
Electron Configuration [Xe]6s24f10
Oxidation States +3
Electronegativity (Pauling Scale) 1.22
Atomic Radius (van der Waals) 228 pm
Ionization Energy 5.939 eV
Melting Point 1685 K
Boiling Point 2840 K
Density 8.55 g/cm³
Year discovered 1886


Dysprosium was discovered in 1886 by Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran in Paris. Its discovery came as a result of research into yttrium oxide, first made in 1794, and from which other rare earths (aka lanthanoids) were subsequently to be extracted, namely erbium in 1843, then holmium in 1878, and finally dysprosium.It is a bright, silvery metallic element.It can be extracted from these minerals by ion exchange and solvent extraction. It can also be prepared by the reduction of dysprosium trifluoride with calcium metal.
