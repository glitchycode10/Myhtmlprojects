Selenium

Standard State Solid
Atomic Mass 78.97 u
Electron Configuration [Ar]4s23d104p4
Oxidation States +6,+4,-2
Electronegativity (Pauling Scale) 2.55
Atomic Radius (van der Waals) 190 pm
Ionization Energy 9.752 eV
Electron Affinity 2.021 eV
Melting Point 493.65 K
Boiling Point 958 K
Density 4.809 g/cm³
Year discovered 1817


Selenium was discovered by Jöns Jacob Berzelius at Stockholm in 1817. He had shares in a sulfuric acid works and he was intrigued by a red-brown sediment which collected at the bottom of the chambers in which the acid was made. It is a semi-metal that can exist in two forms: as a silvery metal or as a red powder.Selenium is found in a few rare minerals. Most of the world’s selenium is obtained from the anode muds produced during the electrolytic refining of copper.
