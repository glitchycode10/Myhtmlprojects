Strontium metal itself was isolated in 1808 at the Royal Institution in London by Humphry Davy by means of electrolysis, using the method with which he had already isolated sodium and potassium. It is a soft, silvery metal that burns in air and reacts with water.Strontium is found mainly in the minerals celestite and strontianite. China is now the leading producer of strontium.
