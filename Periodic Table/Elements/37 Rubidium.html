The lithium potassium mineral lepidolite was discovered in the 1760s and it behaved oddly. When thrown on to glowing coals it frothed and then hardened like glass. Analysis showed it to contain lithium and potassium, but it held a secret: rubidium.A sample of pure rubidium metal was eventually produced in 1928. It is a soft metal that ignites in the air and reacts violently with water.Rubidium occurs in the minerals pollucite, carnallite, leucite and lepidolite.
