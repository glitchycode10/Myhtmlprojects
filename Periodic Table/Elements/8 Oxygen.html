Ionization Energy 13.618 eV
Electron Affinity 54.36 K
Melting Point 54.36 K
Boiling Point 90.2 K
Density 0.001429 g/cm³
Year discovered 1774


The credit for discovering oxygen is now shared by three chemists: an Englishman, a Swede, and a Frenchman. Joseph Priestley was the first to publish an account of oxygen, having made it in 1774 by focussing sunlight on to mercuric oxide (HgO), and collecting the gas which came off.It is a colourless, odourless gas. Oxygen makes up 21% of the atmosphere by volume.The element and its compounds make up 49.2% by mass of the Earth’s crust, and about two-thirds of the human body.
