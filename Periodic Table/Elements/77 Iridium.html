Iridium was discovered together with osmium in1803 by Smithson Tennant in London. It is almost as unreactive as gold. It has a very high density and melting point.Iridium is one of the rarest elements on Earth. It is found uncombined in nature in sediments that were deposited by rivers. It is commercially recovered as a by-product of nickel refining.
